Step 1

Go to musescore.org and download MuseScore Studio.

Step 2

Launch MuseScore Studio and click "New score."

Step 3

Select the instruments you want to use. The "Family" dropdown box allows you to select instruments that are typically used in particular groups, such as marching band instruments. In addition, MuseScore offers pre-made template groups, which can be found under "Create from template."

Step 4

Here, there are palettes available that allow you to add various musical notations. If you cannot find what you are looking for, then you may want to look under "Add palettes," use the search bar, or create a custom palette under "Add palettes."

Step 5

This tab shows you the instruments you have added to your sheet. You can toggle visibility and change the number of staff lines for an instrument here.

Step 6

This tab allows you to change page settings, style settings, and other properties when you select an element.

Step 7

"Parts" and "Mixer" can be found at the top-center of the screen.

Step 8

The "Parts" tab allows you to open pages for each individual part.

Step 9

The "Mixer" tab allows you to adjust the volume of each part. Note that this is only within the program and does not apply like a dynamic change.

Step 10

This can be found near the top of the page and allows you to select note divisions and other general elements.

Step 11

The "Publish" page can be found in the top-left corner of the window.

Step 12

The "Export" window allows you to select parts and save them as a particular format.
